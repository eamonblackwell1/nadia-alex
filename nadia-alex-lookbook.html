Your Vision

You want your guests to feel two sides of Tokyo.

Thursday night is the city at its most electric. A rooftop, the skyline, cold drinks, neon glow. Lost in Translation energy. Everyone lands, loosens up, and remembers why they flew across the world for this.

Saturday is the centrepiece. A Japanese garden, an elegant reception room, a live band that keeps everyone on the dance floor until 10pm. Romantic but never stuffy. Traditional but with your personality running through every detail. Sake barrel ceremonies, yakitori stations, purikura booths, roving sake shots.

Sunday is the exhale. Something relaxed and fun. A pool party if you can swing it, or an izakaya takeover, or a lazy afternoon in the park. The kind of morning-after that makes people not want to leave.

You've lived here for eight years. You know Tokyo. This wedding is your chance to show your guests the city you love, through both its energy and its elegance.

Sunday

The Recovery

Budget permitting, this is the day that turns a wedding into a weekend. You mentioned wanting a pool party. We'll be honest: most Tokyo hotel pools don't open until July, and private hire of a hotel pool for 90 guests is very difficult. But there is one realistic path, plus a great alternative we think you'll love.

Pool Option — Worth Exploring

ANA InterContinental Tokyo

Their outdoor garden pool officially opens June 1, but has opened as early as late May in previous years. Because late May is pre-season, the pool would otherwise be sitting empty, which makes a private arrangement more realistic than trying to block off an active pool. 20m pool with Tokyo Tower views, poolside bar, and space for your group. We'd reach out to their events team to explore a bespoke arrangement.

Our Alternative Pick

Private Yacht on Tokyo Bay

A private charter for 90 guests cruising past Rainbow Bridge and Tokyo Tower. Open sky decks, BBQ, free-flow drinks, and DJ or playlist on board. Same relaxed waterfront energy as a pool party, but completely private by definition. Prices start from around ¥900,000≈ A$8,010 total including food and drinks. A 2–3 hour afternoon cruise the day after the wedding is hard to beat.

The Easy Win

Izakaya Takeover

Book out a great izakaya for a long, lazy group lunch. Yakitori, cold beer, and the kind of afternoon that nobody wants to end. Low logistics, high fun. This is always a crowd-pleaser and easy to organise at any budget level.

Also on the Table

Park Picnic

Yoyogi Park or Shinjuku Gyoen. Blankets, bento boxes, drinks. Easy, beautiful, and very Tokyo in late spring.

 

Rooftop Brunch

A relaxed late-morning gathering at a rooftop venue. Same city views as Thursday night, but in the sunshine. Easy to combine with a farewell.
